Primary Goal: Collect practitioner insights on real-world challenges in managing diverse teams, including resistance patterns, barriers, and why many diversity initiatives fail.
Secondary Goal: Identify diversity management and training practices that have demonstrated measurable improvements in performance, engagement, and turnover.

Target Practitioners
Industry Experts (Preferred Profile)
- Experience: 5–15+ years in HR, DEI, talent development, or organizational psychology
- Roles: CDOs, HR Directors, DEI Managers, OD specialists, HR Business Partners
- Industries: Tech, healthcare, finance, professional services, manufacturing
- Org Size: 500+ employees
- Location: U.S.-based practitioners, especially from diverse metro areas

Practitioner Types to Interview

- HR Director or University/Fortune 500 HR Leader
    - Insight: Implementation challenges, training effectiveness, performance metrics
    - Contact via: LinkedIn, alumni network, SHRM chapters
- DEI Consultant (Deloitte, McKinsey, Accenture)
    - Insight: Cross-industry patterns, benchmarking, best practices
    - Contact via: Conferences, LinkedIn outreach
- Chief Diversity Officer (Tech/Healthcare)
    - Insight: Effective programs, ROI measurement, scaling
    - Contact via: Industry associations, webinars
- HR Partner from Manufacturing/Construction
    - Insight: Barriers in low-adoption, traditional industries
    - Contact via: Trade groups, LinkedIn
- Practitioner Featured in Case Studies
    - Insight: Firsthand experience with proven programs
    - Contact via: HBR/SHRM authors, company DEI contacts
- Academic Practitioner (Professor-Consultant)
    - Insight: Evidence-based recommendations, theory–practice integration
    - Contact via: Faculty directories, research authors

Acquisition Methods
Practitioner Interviews
- Target: 6–8 interviews (at least one each from high-performing and low-performing industries)
- Format: 30–45 minutes via Zoom/Teams
- Data Capture: Audio recording with consent + detailed notes
- Output: Thematic coding linked to Peterson Framework

Interview Structure:
- Background: Role, experience, industries served
- X (Challenges): Conflict, turnover, failed initiatives, cultural barriers
- M (Training & Solutions): What works, what doesn't, program elements that matter
- Y (Outcomes): Impact on performance, engagement, retention; ROI timelines

Professional Networks & Outreach

LinkedIn Strategy:
- Search terms: CDO, HR Director, DEI Manager, Talent Development
- Personalized messages connecting their experience to your research
- Aim for warm introductions via 2nd-degree contacts

Associations to Leverage:
- SHRM, National Diversity Council, ATD, HR People + Strategy, Catalyst
- Attend webinars and follow-up with speakers during Q&A

Case Study Research Strategy

Sources
- HBR, MIT Sloan, SHRM, McKinsey, Deloitte
- Diversity reports from high-performing companies (Salesforce, Intel, Microsoft, J&J)
- DiversityInc, SHRM award winners
- Documented failures for comparison

Documentation Template (Condensed)
- X: Diversity challenge
- M: Training/intervention details
- Y: Outcomes (turnover, engagement, innovation, performance)
- Timeline: When results appeared
- Success Factors & Challenges
- Applicability & Limitations

Documentation & Analysis

Interviews
- Record (with consent) + take structured notes
- Transcribe key quotes only
- Code evidence using Peterson Framework:
    - Critical incidents
    - Feedback quality
    - Environmental consistency

Case Studies
- Use consistent template
- Evaluate source credibility
- Extract quantitative evidence

Outreach Plan & Timeline
- Start outreach: Week 1
- Interview window: 3 weeks
- Follow-ups: 1–2 polite reminders
- Buffer: Final week reserved for clarifications and analysis

Expected Challenges & Mitigation

- Access Difficulty
    - Senior DEI leaders are busy and DEI can be politically sensitive
    - Mitigation: Broaden outreach, use referrals, target mid-level practitioners too
- Low Response Rates
    - Typical 10–20%
    - Mitigation: Personalize outreach, multi-channel contact, follow-up politely
- Quality of Insights
    - Risk of vague or overly positive responses
    - Mitigation: Ask for specifics, metrics, failures, and concrete examples
- Time Constraints
    - Short window for interviews and transcription
    - Mitigation: Begin early, prioritize high-value contacts, use partial transcripts
