**What Are "Bias Interrupters"? Concrete Examples**

**Definition:** Structural processes that reduce bias in talent decisions without relying solely on manager goodwill or awareness. These are system-level guardrails embedded in workflows.

**Hiring Bias Interrupters:**
1. **Standardized interview guides:** 8 pre-defined behavioral questions, 1-5 scoring rubric, no "tell me about yourself" freeform questions (reduces favoritism based on rapport)
2. **Diverse interview panels:** Minimum 2 interviewers per candidate, at least 1 from underrepresented group, separate scoring before discussion (reduces groupthink)
3. **Blind resume review:** Remove names, schools, graduation years from initial screening (reduces prestige bias, age bias, ethnicity assumptions)
4. **Skills-based assessments:** Work sample tests or case studies evaluated before personality interviews (predicts performance better than unstructured interviews)

**Performance Evaluation Bias Interrupters:**
1. **Calibration meetings:** Managers present draft ratings to peers + skip-level leader, justify scores with behavioral evidence before finalizing (reduces leniency bias, halo effects)
2. **Behavioral rating anchors:** Each competency defined with 1-5 behavioral descriptions (e.g., "Strategic Thinking Level 3: Identifies quarterly risks, proposes mitigation plans" vs. vague "somewhat strategic")
3. **Forced distribution limits:** No more than 30% "exceeds expectations" ratings per team (prevents rating inflation for favored employees)
4. **Demographic audits:** People Analytics reviews rating distributions by demographics before final approval, flags >0.3-point gaps (triggers manager re-review)

**Assignment & Promotion Bias Interrupters:**
1. **Transparent criteria:** High-visibility projects require documented business case + skill fit assessment, posted for nominations (no informal "tap on the shoulder" assignments)
2. **Rotation tracking:** Dashboard shows assignment distribution by demographics, alerts when >20% gap (e.g., if 40% employees are diverse but only 25% get stretch assignments)
3. **Promotion slates:** Leadership reviews promotion-ready pipeline quarterly, requires diverse candidate slates before external hiring for senior roles (prevents "we couldn't find anyone" excuses)
4. **Sponsorship requirements:** Directors+ required to sponsor 1-2 diverse employees annually with documented advocacy actions (attend calibration, nominate for projects, introduce to execs)

**Meeting & Collaboration Bias Interrupters:**
1. **Round-robin speaking:** Facilitator calls on quiet voices first, uses chat for async input, rotates meeting leadership (prevents loudest voices from dominating)
2. **Idea attribution:** Meeting notes explicitly credit who raised each idea (prevents "Sarah said X, then Brian repeated X and got credit")
3. **Pre-reads + think time:** Share materials 24 hours before meeting, start with silent review time (helps non-native speakers, introverts, neurodivergent employees)

**Why Structural Interrupters Matter:**
- Training changes awareness; interrupters change systems
- Removes burden from diverse employees to self-advocate
- Makes bias visible through data (can't improve what you don't measure)
- Sustains behavior change after training ends (habits reinforced by workflow design)
